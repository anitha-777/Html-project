Aloo Gobi (Potato & Cauliflower Curry)


  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 25–30 minutes
  • Total Time: ~45 minutes
  • Servings: 3–4
  • Cuisine: North Indian
  • Difficulty: Easy

    • 📋 Ingredients:
    🧾 Main:
  • 2 medium potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • small cauliflower (cut into medium florets)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 1–2 tomatoes (chopped or pureed)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
    • 🌶️ Spices:
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
    • Optional: 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
    🔥 Steps to Cook:
      🥦 1. Prep the Vegetables Wash and chop potatoes and cauliflower into similar-sized pieces. Optionally, blanch cauliflower in hot water for 3–4 mins to remove worms.
      🍳 2. Sauté the Base Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1–2 mins.
      🍅 3. Add Tomatoes & Spices Add chopped/pureed tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy. Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook until oil begins to separate.
      🥔 4. Cook the Veggies Add potatoes and cauliflower. Stir well to coat them with the masala. Cover and cook on low-medium flame for 15–20 minutes. Stir occasionally and sprinkle water if it starts to stick. Cook until potatoes and cauliflower are soft but not mushy.
      🌿 5. Finish & Serve Add garam masala and amchur powder (if using). Mix gently. Turn off the heat. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
      🍽️ Best Served With: Roti, paratha, or plain rice Dal or raita on the side for a complete meal
    💡 Tips:
  • Don’t overcook cauliflower — it should hold its shape.
  • For a richer taste, add a spoon of ghee or kasuri methi at the end.

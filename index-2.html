Paneer Butter Masala
  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 30 minutes
  • Total Time: 45 minutes
  • Serving Size: 2–3 people
  • Cuisine: North Indian
  • Difficulty: Easy

    • 📋 Ingredients:
    🧀 For the Curry:
  • 200g paneer (cut into cubes)
  • 3 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp fresh cream (optional for richness)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

    • 🌶️ Spices:
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

    • 🔥 Steps to Cook:
      🔪 1. Prep the Ingredients Chop onions and tomatoes. Puree the tomatoes in a blender. Cut the paneer into cubes (lightly pan-fry if you prefer firmer texture).
      🍳 2. Make the Curry Base Heat oil and 1 tbsp butter in a pan on medium heat. Add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions. Sauté onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste. Cook until raw smell disappears. Pour in the tomato puree. Stir well and cook until oil separates from the sides (takes 7–8 minutes). Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
      🥘 3. Add Paneer and Final Touches Add 1/2 cup water (adjust based on desired thickness). Add paneer cubes gently into the curry. Sprinkle garam masala, sugar, and kasuri methi (crushed between palms). Simmer for 5–7 minutes so paneer absorbs the flavors. Stir in fresh cream and the remaining 1 tbsp butter for a rich, smooth finish. 🌿 Garnish & Serve Garnish with coriander leaves (optional). Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

    Tips
  • Cream Substitute: Use milk if you don’t have cream.

  • Restaurant Style: Add a few drops of honey and a pinch of cardamom powder for that extra touch.

  • Vegan Option: Replace paneer with tofu and butter/cream with plant-based alternatives.

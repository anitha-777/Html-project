Chole Masala (Chickpea Curry)



Here’s a detailed recipe for Chole Masala (Chickpea Curry) – a hearty, spicy, and flavorful North Indian dish loved across India!

🍲 Chole Masala (Chickpea Curry)
  • Prep Time: 15 minutes (excluding soaking time)
  • Cook Time: 30–40 minutes
  • Total Time: ~1 hour
  • Serving Size: 3–4 people
  • Cuisine: North Indian
  • Difficulty: Moderate

    • 📋 Ingredients:
    🧾 Main:
  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
    • 🌶️ Spices:
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice
  • 1 black tea bag (for color – optional)
    • 🔥 Steps to Cook:
      🫘 1. Soak & Cook Chickpeas Soak 1 cup dried chickpeas overnight in water (at least 8 hours). Drain and rinse. Add to a pressure cooker with 3 cups water and a tea bag (for darker color – optional). Pressure cook for 4–5 whistles or until chickpeas are soft. Remove tea bag after cooking. If using canned chickpeas, skip this step and just rinse them.
      🍳 2. Prepare the Masala Base Heat oil in a pan on medium heat. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1–2 minutes. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates from the mixture.n
      🌶️ 3. Add Spices Add turmeric, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook the masala for 2–3 minutes until well mixed and aromatic.
      🥘 4. Combine with Chickpeas Add the cooked chickpeas (along with some of the water they were boiled in). Mix everything and mash a few chickpeas for thicker gravy. Add more water if needed and simmer for 10–15 minutes. Add garam masala and amchur (or lemon juice) at the end.
    🌿 Garnish & Serve
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and sliced onions.
  • Serve hot with bhature, poori, jeera rice, or roti.
    • 💡 Tips:
  • Add a pinch of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) for restaurant-style flavor.
  • For creaminess, stir in 1 tbsp fresh cream or a small piece of boiled potato.
  • You can use store-bought chole masala powder instead of individual spices.

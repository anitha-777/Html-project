Rajma Masala


Here’s a detailed recipe for Rajma Masala (Kidney Bean Curry) – a comforting and protein-rich North Indian dish, perfect with rice!
🍛 Rajma Masala (Kidney Bean Curry)
  • Prep Time: 15 minutes (excluding soaking time)
  • Cook Time: 40–45 minutes
  • Total Time: ~1 hour
  • Servings: 3–4 people
  • Cuisine: North Indian
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
    • 📋 Ingredients: 🫘 Main:
  • 1 cup dried rajma (kidney beans)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

    • 🌶️ Spices:
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1–2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1.5 tsp coriander powder
  • 1tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Optional: 1 tsp rajma masala (store-bought)
    • 🔥 Cooking Steps:
      1️. Soak and Cook Rajma Rinse and soak rajma overnight (or 8 hours) in enough water. Pressure cook with 3–4 cups water and salt for 5–6 whistles, or until soft. It should mash easily between your fingers. If using canned rajma, rinse and use directly.
      2.Prepare the Masala Base Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and bay leaves. Let them crackle. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell disappears. Add tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and oil separates.
      3 Add Spices Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Sauté well for 2–3 minutes until the masala is cooked through.
      4 Combine Rajma and Simmer Add cooked rajma with its water (or add fresh water for gravy consistency). Simmer on low-medium heat for 15–20 minutes to allow flavors to blend. Slightly mash a few rajma beans to thicken the gravy.
      5 Finish with Garam Masala Add garam masala and amchur (or lemon juice). Mix well and cook for another 2–3 minutes. Turn off the heat and rest for 5 mins before serving.
    🌿 Garnish & Serve
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice, jeera rice, or roti.
    • 💡 Tips:
  • Use kashmiri chili powder for color without too much heat.
  • A spoon of fresh cream or butter can make it richer.
